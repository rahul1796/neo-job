About the Report
This report provides an in-depth description of your responses to the Career Assessment. Designed to
help you identify and understand your potential, personality and career interests, this report can start
you on the journey of matching your attributes with your academic and career goals and to understand
your capabilities and compare yourself against skills that employers want. This report is designed to
expand your options, rather than limit them, thereby providing you many avenues to explore.

How to interpret and use your Career Assessment Report:
Knowing your interests is important because it allows you to make informed career decisions and
indicates work that you will enjoy. People whose interests match their occupations and activities find
greater satisfaction, are more productive, and have higher levels of motivation. These results can
increase your chances for career success.
Report Analysis:
This report will show you which careers are a good match for you. It aids you to identify your interests
related to a work environment and provide clarity in the vocational choices most suited to you. Aligning
these Interest findings with career choices is likely to facilitate greater work satisfaction, higher
motivation and improved productivity.
Career Interest Inventory Report
Interest Types:
Work environments can be divided into the six basic types of work or a combination of these types. Your
personal career type will be a combination of two or three of these fundamental types of work. It will be
represented by a two or three letter code. A brief description of each of the five Work Areas is shown
below:
Mechanical: interest in working mainly with hands by making, fixing, assembling, and constructing
things, using and operating an equipment, tools, or machines. They often like to work outdoors.
Investigative:  interest in working mainly with collecting and analyzing data and information. They are
curious and tend to be creative and original. Investigative types are task oriented, and tend to prefer
loosely structured situations with minimal rules or regulations.
Creative: interest in jobs involving artistic, innovative or intuitive abilities, and like to work with ideas,
abstractions, and concepts in unstructured creative situations.
Service Oriented: interest to work with people to inform, help, train, enlighten, develop or cure or are
skilled with words. They like tasks that involve teamwork, social interaction, relationship building, and
improvement of society
Sales: interest in jobs which require working with people to influence, perform, persuade, or lead for
organizational goals or economic gain. They usually like meeting people, leading, talking to and
influencing others, encourage others and work in business.
Administration: interest in jobs that involve working with data, clerical tasks, or numerical ability,
carrying things out in detail or following instructions. They like structured work environments where
they can follow clear procedures to organize information.
